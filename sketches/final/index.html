Sleep and Caffeine Visualization Sketches

These sketches propose three different visualization approaches for my sleep and caffeine dataset. The project explores how caffeine intake, stress level, and bedtime may affect sleep latency and sleep duration. Each sketch includes labeled axes, units, approximate ranges, and a legend.

Sketch 1 – Scatter / Bubble Plot

Hypothesis: As the gap between caffeine intake and bedtime decreases, sleep latency increases. Higher stress levels may also increase sleep latency.

Data used: Gap Before Bed (hours, approx. 10–18), Sleep Latency (minutes, approx. 3–50), Stress Level (scale 1–5), Caffeine Amount (mg, approx. 40–100).

Representation: The x-axis shows Gap Before Bed in hours. The y-axis shows Sleep Latency in minutes. Bubble size represents Stress Level. Bubble shading represents Caffeine Amount.

Sketch 2 – Scatterplot with Shapes

Hypothesis: Higher caffeine consumption may reduce total sleep duration. Stress level may also affect sleep duration.

Data used: Caffeine Amount (mg, approx. 40–100), Sleep Duration (hours, approx. 6.0–8.5), Caffeine Time (10am, 1pm, 3pm), Stress Level (scale 1–5).

Representation: The x-axis shows Caffeine Amount in milligrams. The y-axis shows Sleep Duration in hours. Different shapes represent Caffeine Time. Different shading patterns represent Stress Level.

Sketch 3 – Bedtime vs. Sleep Latency

Hypothesis: Later bedtimes are associated with longer sleep latency. Caffeine amount may increase this effect.

Data used: Bedtime (hours after midnight, approx. 0.5–4.0), Sleep Latency (minutes, approx. 3–50), Caffeine Amount (categories from 0 mg to 100 mg).

Representation: The x-axis shows Bedtime in hours after midnight. The y-axis shows Sleep Latency in minutes. Circle color categories represent different Caffeine Amounts.
